Authors: Gaurav, Mubeen, Vinu, Srikanth¶

This project aims at analyzing the mass shootings incidents occurred in the United States between 1982-2019.¶

We have analyzed the different aspects of data including location, number of incidents/year, number of victims, latitude, longitude to come up with visualizations and gain meaningful insights from the data.

INTRODUCTION:¶

This dataset provides information on mass shooting occurences since the year 1982. The dataset has 115 rows and 24 columns of information. The data is diverse and provides as host of information ranging from the location to fatalities and total victims to information pertaining to the shooter like his age, prior mental health issues, race ,gender, etc.

We chose this dataset as mass shootings are a relevant issue today in the USA, with many lives being lost due to acts of gun violence by obnoxious people. Our aim with the project was to see patterns in the data like the race of the shooter, increase in the incident rate over the years and why this is an issue that needs to be seriously addressed.

Below, we are going to see how the information from the dataset can be used to create meaningful data visualizations that convey important facts and figures from the dataset to the viewers, who in this case do not have good insight into the data themselves.

DATASET DESCRIPTION AND DEFINITIONS¶

The dataset consists of Mass shooting occuring in the US from 1982 to 2019. The dataset is a collection of discriminate rampages in public places resulting in four or more victims killed by the shooter.¶

It does not include shootings resulting from more primitive incidents such as armed robbery or gang violence.

It also includes interesting information about the registered case, location, weapin used, vicitms, number of persons injured and detailed about the shooter age, race, gender and whether the shooter had prior sign of mental health issues.

The columns are as follows:

  • Case:- It contains the name of the shooting registered in the dataset.

  • location:- It contains the name of different state location within the United states where shooting happened.

  • date:- Contains the date and year of the shooting.

  • summary:- It contains the detail information about the shooting, what are the reasons behind the shooting and how many people were killed.

  • fatalities:- It contains the information for an occurrence of death in mass shooting.

  • injured:- Contains number of people injured in shooting.

  • total_victims:- Contains the information about the total number of victims present while shooting.

  • location_2:- It also contains the location of shooting happened in different location like school and workplace.

  • age_of_shooter:- Information about the age of shooter.

  • prior_signs_mental_health_issues:- It contains information about any mental sign of the shooter.

  • mental_health_details:- Contains details of mental health detail of shooter, like whether the mental condition of shooter was good or bad before he had done the crime.

  • weapons_obtained_legally:- Information of the weapons obtained by shooter is legall or inlegall.

  • where_obtained:- Information from where shooter purchased or obtained the weapon.

  • weapon_type:- Detailed information about the typer of weapon used, like whether its a handgun or shotgun.

  • race:- Information of the shooter belongs to which racial division or ethic group

  • gender:- Contains information whether shooter is male or female.

  • sources:- It contains information from where we got the shooting report, whether from news channel or blogs.

  • type:- It contains the information about the type of shooting. Whether its Sperr which involve two or more victim died and Mass which involve mutliple victims or groups of peoples killed in gun shooting.

  • year:- Information in which year shooting happend.

Visualization 1: Number of mass shootings grouped by gender¶

We wanted to check the gender breakdown of the shooters for all the years between 1982 - 2019. The first visualization is an interactive bar plot showing the gender breakdown of the shooters for all shootings. We used Pyplot package to make use of the interactive features.

Out[7]:
Text(0.5, 1.0, 'Number of mass shootings grouped by gender across years')

Explanation and Inferences¶

The dataset was first grouped by year and gender for plotting. We then created a list of values for all the 3 categories mentioned in the gender and supplied this list as a parameter while plotting the bar graph. We can hover over the bar and it will show the year and the exact count.

We see that around 97% of the shootings (Men total = 111, Female total = 3, Others total = 1) have been caused by Men. This knowledge combined with the mental health data can be used to predict the likelihood of a person getting involved in a shooting.

Explanation and conclusion¶

This plot depicts the various locations across US where mass shooting incidents have occurred from 1982-2019. The radius of each circle represent the severity of the incident factored by a scalar value. The severity of incident is decided by the total number of victims of that incident. On click of each of the circle, we see details of the incident such as the location, case reported, total victims and the year the case occurred.

It can be seen from the plot that the largest mass shooting occurred in Las Vegas with case reported as Las Vegas Strip Massacre which saw total victims of 604 in the year 2017. We can also infer that a significant amount of mass shootings have taken place in California (San Jose, Sacramento, Las Vegas, Los Angeles) and Florida.

Visualization 7: Exploring widgets for scatterplot¶

This visualization aimed at providing a UI widget with flexibility for the user to pick and observe the trend of various mass shooting observatory data against other parameters.

Explanation and conclusion¶

The visualization above made use of two ipywidgets of type Dropdown to display various categorical values that can be used for plotting. The plotting was done using the scatter function of the bqplot and all the 3 components were displayed using the VBox function of the ipywidgets

Few interesting inferences in the data through these plots:

  • Majority of shooters were in the middle age i.e. between 25-45 however the minimum age observed was around 11 and the maximum age observed was 65+
  • An average number of fatilities in these incidents have been between 2-15 however, went high in the 2017 incident in Las Vegas where the reported number was 58.
